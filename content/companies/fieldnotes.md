User research has a distribution problem.

The insights exist. They live in Notion pages and Google Docs and Dovetail boards and, most persistently, in the heads of the two or three researchers who did the work. Product managers know they should consult them. The researchers know they're not being consulted. Both parties are too busy to fix the gap.

Fieldnotes was built to close it.

Our product was well-reviewed. Our customers were loyal, articulate advocates who would demo Fieldnotes to other teams at their companies with a conviction that made our sales team feel, briefly, that everything was going to work out.

Enterprise procurement was the problem. Fieldnotes was a departmental buy in a world where procurement increasingly wanted platform solutions. Our natural champion — the head of research — rarely had budget authority. Our buyer — the VP of Product — was easier to reach but harder to convince to add another SaaS line item.

We were acquired by Panoply Software in 2024. The press release was enthusiastic. The integration into Panoply's platform was described as "in progress" on the roadmap through Q3, then "deprioritized" in Q4, then removed from the public roadmap without announcement.

The Fieldnotes team is distributed across Panoply's organization now. The product is technically still accessible to existing customers. The last commit to the codebase was eight months ago.
