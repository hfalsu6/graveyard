Verdant was founded on a simple premise: the food system is broken, and the fix is a well-designed subscription box.

We spent eighteen months building proprietary farm-matching software, a cold-chain logistics network that required five separate vendor contracts, and a brand identity that cost more than our first six months of inventory.

Our customers were enthusiastic. Our retention was not. The average Verdant subscriber stayed for 2.3 months before discovering that cooking with obscure heritage grains on a Wednesday night is aspirational rather than practical.

We pivoted to "flexible frequency" in Q3 2021. We launched a corporate gifting line in Q1 2022. We explored a white-label partnership with a regional grocery chain in Q2 2022. In Q3 2022, we explored bankruptcy.

The farms are still there. The mycelium packaging has fully composted. We have not.
