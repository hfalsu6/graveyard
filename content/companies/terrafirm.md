TerraFirm believed that real estate wealth should not be limited to people who can afford a down payment. This was a genuine conviction, not a pitch deck sentiment.

The regulatory environment for tokenized real estate was, at the time of our founding, best described as "actively forming." We engaged securities counsel early. We filed our Reg A+ offering. We disclosed everything in a document that our compliance team was proud of and our users did not read.

The SEC's inquiry in late 2021 concerned our secondary market. Specifically, it concerned whether the secondary market constituted an unregistered securities exchange. It also concerned certain representations made in marketing materials that our legal team had approved and our growth team had enthusiastically improved.

We cooperated fully with the inquiry. The inquiry took fourteen months. We settled without admitting wrongdoing, which our lawyers described as a good outcome and which felt, experientially, like the other kind.

TerraFirm is no longer accepting investments. The properties still exist. The tenants are fine.
