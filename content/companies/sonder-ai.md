The insight behind Sonder was real: organizations are bad at catching burnout until it becomes attrition, and attrition is expensive. Every HR leader we spoke to nodded vigorously when we described the problem.

The product worked. That was part of the problem.

Our NLP model flagged a VP of Engineering at a Fortune 500 pilot customer as "at significant attrition risk" in February 2022. The VP left in March 2022. The customer loved this. The VP did not.

We spent six months navigating the gap between "this is powerful" and "this is terrifying." We added more privacy controls. We softened the language in manager digests. We rebranded from "emotional surveillance" to "team health observability," which helped with sales and did nothing for the underlying tension.

In 2023, we were acquired by Horizon Labs for our engineering team. The product was sunset 48 hours after close. The acqui-hire came with a 24-month retention package and a strict NDA.

Three of the four founders still work there. One left after nine months. We did not need an AI to see that coming.
