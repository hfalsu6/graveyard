We launched in March 2020. The timing was, depending on your perspective, either perfectly aligned with the remote work revolution or evidence of dangerous overconfidence.

Both things were true.

2020 and 2021 were extraordinary. Occupancy rates across our portfolio averaged 91%. Our waitlist in Lisbon reached 400 people. We raised our Series A on a Zoom call and our Series B on a different Zoom call. We expanded from 8 cities to 28 in 18 months.

The community managers were the heart of it. When they were good — and most of them were extraordinary — they transformed a furnished apartment into something that felt like belonging to something. We had members who renewed year after year, following the network from city to city.

In 2022, the remote work consensus began to fracture. Return-to-office mandates hit our member base harder than we modeled. Occupancy dropped to 74%, then 68%. We closed our weakest markets. We cut the community manager program in four cities to reduce costs. The cancellations in those cities accelerated.

We had built a product where the economics and the soul of the thing were in direct tension. When we cut costs, we cut the soul. When we cut the soul, we lost the members.

In 2023, we sold our remaining lease portfolio to a traditional furnished-rental operator. They kept the name. They did not keep the community managers.
